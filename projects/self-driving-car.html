Random agent vs A.I.

After taking an artificial intelligence course, I decided to try my hands on training a self-driving car on a simulated environment.


The goal of the self-driving agent is to reach its destination within the alloted amount of time and does so without violating any traffic rules. The agent is trained on the U.S. right of way traffic. For instance, it needs to learn to not make a left turn when there is incoming traffic even though the traffic light is green!


The results below show that if the agent acted randomly, it would almost always fail to get to its destination! For instance, in the GIF below, you can see that the agent (white car) attempts to move forward even when the light is red! How dangerous!


But the intelligent agent almost always achieves its goal, safely!

Relation with Psychology

In Psychology, reinforcement is defined as a process of increasing the likelihood an organism to manifest a particular behavior when given a set of stimuli. For instance, rewarding oneself with a piece of chocolate after a grueling workout is a type of positive reinforcement. It reinforces workout with chocolate and hence one is more likely to workout in the future.

The same notion is applied to A.I. in training a self-driving car/agent. The goal of the agent is to learn how to drive without violating any traffic rules. In the training phase, when it violates a rule, it is heavily penalized. In the process of reaching its destination, if it correctly obeys a rule, it is rewarded. As soon as it reaches its destination, it is given a much bigger reward.

With enough exploration, exploitation, it will learn and remember (using computer memory storage) the optimal policy (way to act in the environment).
