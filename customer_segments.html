Implementation: Feature Relevance¶

One interesting thought to consider is if one (or more) of the six product categories is actually relevant for understanding customer purchasing. That is to say, is it possible to determine whether customers purchasing some amount of one category of products will necessarily purchase some proportional amount of another category of products? We can make this determination quite easily by training a supervised regression learner on a subset of the data with one feature removed, and then score how well that model can predict the removed feature.

In the code block below, you will need to implement the following:

  • Assign new_data a copy of the data by removing a feature of your choice using the DataFrame.drop function.

Implementation: Outlier Detection¶

Detecting outliers in the data is extremely important in the data preprocessing step of any analysis. The presence of outliers can often skew results which take into consideration these data points. There are many "rules of thumb" for what constitutes an outlier in a dataset. Here, we will use Tukey's Method for identfying outliers: An outlier step is calculated as 1.5 times the interquartile range (IQR). A data point with a feature that is beyond an outlier step outside of the IQR for that feature is considered abnormal.

  • Assign the calculation of an outlier step for the given feature to step.
  • Optionally remove data points from the dataset by adding indices to the outliers list.

NOTE: If you choose to remove any outliers, ensure that the sample data does not contain any of these points!

Question 6¶

What are the advantages to using a K-Means clustering algorithm? What are the advantages to using a Gaussian Mixture Model clustering algorithm? Given your observations about the wholesale customer data so far, which of the two algorithms will you use and why?

Answer:

Advantages of K-Means: K-Means clusters data by trying to separate samples into k clusters with equal variance, minimizing inertia. Inertia responds poorly to elongated clusters and is not a normalized metric. Running PCA can alleviate this issue. It scales well to large datasets. Given enough time, K-Means always converges, albeit to local minimum sometimes (depending on initialization of centroids).

Advantages of Gaussian Mixture Model: The likelihood is monotonically non-decreasing and it will never diverge. Configurations for EM are probabilities, so it's difficult to converge to the global optimum. However, it can get stuck, but as with K-Means, we can restart.

Choice: Since after scaling each feature is approximately normal, I have decided to use GMM. The reason is that for GMM, points (customers) can be shared between more than one cluster.

Implementation: Creating Clusters¶

The silhouette coefficient for a data point measures how similar it is to its assigned cluster from -1 (dissimilar) to 1 (similar). How would you use the customer segments you found above to perform an A/B Test for this change?

Answer: For each segment, we can randomly divide the customers into control and variation groups. As implied by the names, the customers in the control group will continue to have their products delivered to them 5 days/week; customers in the variation group will get their products 3 days/week. Then, for each segment, we compute the observed significance level, a.k.a the P-value. After determining the threshold, we can conclude that the testing data support the null hypothesis if the P-value is greater than the threshold. If the P-values for both segments exceed the threshold, then, it's plausible to roll out the new delivery service to both segments. But, we can also determine the group that's affected the most from the magnitude of the P-value. The segment with greater P-value gets affected the most.
